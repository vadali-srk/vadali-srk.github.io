Dr. Siva Ram Krishna Vadali

Scientist–G | CSIR–Central Mechanical Engineering Research Institute
Adjunct Faculty | Academy of Scientific and Innovative Research (AcSIR)

I am a Scientist–G at the CSIR–Central Mechanical Engineering Research Institute (CSIR-CMERI), Durgapur, under the Ministry of Science & Technology, Government of India. My research lies at the intersection of mathematical statistics, statistical signal processing, communication systems, intelligent sensing, artificial intelligence and autonomous engineering systems.

Over the years, I have had the privilege of contributing to strategic, industrial and societal research through projects involving remote sensing, communication systems, buried seismic sensing, intelligent instrumentation and autonomous underwater robotics. My current research aims to bridge rigorous mathematical theory with deployable engineering systems capable of addressing real-world challenges.

Research Philosophy

My research philosophy centres on transforming mathematically rigorous statistical inference techniques into practical engineering systems. While theoretical optimality remains important, I strongly believe that engineering solutions must also satisfy practical constraints associated with computational complexity, real-time implementation and hardware feasibility. This philosophy has guided my work in statistical signal processing, intelligent sensing, underwater robotics and communication systems over the past two decades.

Research Vision

My long-term vision is to develop mathematically grounded technologies for intelligent engineering systems by integrating statistical signal processing, sensing, communication and artificial intelligence. Through interdisciplinary collaborations, I aspire to translate theoretical advances into deployable technologies addressing strategic, industrial and societal needs.
